Is That Brand DM a Scam?

5 red flags to check before you reply to any "collaboration" offer.

Quick Answer

How To Tell It's Fake

Real brands never ask you to pay them. If a "collab" asks for upfront payment, your login details, or has no real website behind it, treat it as a scam until proven otherwise.

Red Flag · 1 & 2

Money Moving the Wrong Way

🚩

Upfront payment request

"Pay for the product, we'll reimburse you" — they won't.

🚩

Login or phishing links

Any link asking for your Instagram password is fake.

Red Flag · 3 & 4

No Real Agreement

🚩

No written contract

Vague terms with nothing in writing about pay or deliverables.

🚩

Too good to be true

Huge pay for one Story post is rarely a real offer.

Red Flag · 5

The Message Itself

🚩

"Hi dear, collab with us?"

Generic, poorly written, no brand name or website mentioned.

What's Real Looks Like

Before You Say Yes

✓

Brand has a real website and socials

✓

Outreach from an official email

✓

Written terms — pay, deadline, deliverables

✓

Never asks you for money or your password

People Also Ask

Quick FAQs

Should I ever pay to join a collab?

No — never. Real brands pay you, not the other way around.

What if I already paid?

Stop contact immediately, screenshot everything, and report the account.
